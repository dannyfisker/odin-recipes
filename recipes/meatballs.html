Spaghetti and meatballs

Spaghetti and meatballs is an Italian-American dish consisting of spaghetti, tomato sauce and meatballs.

Following ingredients is what makes this recipe successful and tasteful.

Ingredients

Spaghetti and meatballs for 4 people

  • 500 g. spaghetti
  • Parmesan cheese

Tomato sauce

  • 3 cans of whole peeled tomatoes
  • 1 small onion
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 tbsp. olive oil
  • 1 tbsp. sugar
  • 1 tsp. coarse salt
  • 1 tsp. oregano
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Meatballs

  • 500 g. mixed minced beef and pork, beef and veal or pure veal
  • 1 small onion
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 egg
  • ½ dl. grate
  • 2 large tbsp. finely grated Parmesan
  • 1 dl. milk
  • The leaves from two sprigs of thyme
  • 1 large tbsp. chopped parsley
  • 1 tsp. fennel seeds, crushed in a mortar
  • ½ tsp. fine salt
  • Black pepper - freshly ground
  • Oil for frying

Steps for making the tomato sauce

  1. Finely chop the onion and garlic.
  2. Use a wide pan, wide enough to put the meatballs in later,
    to fry the onions at moderate heat in the oil for approx. 3 minutes. It must not brown.
  3. Add the garlic and oregano and continue to fry for 1 minute.
  4. Add peeled tomatoes whole. Don't chop.
  5. Add sugar, salt and pepper and let the sauce simmer on low heat for at least 30 minutes.
    If it boils too much, add a bit of water.
  6. Meanwhile, make the meatballs.

Steps for making the meatballs

  1. Finely chop the onion and garlic.
  2. Mix all ingredients together to a firm mince.
  3. Leave it to sit for 10 minutes so the rasp can absorb moisture and the stuffing is not too wet.
  4. Wheel for golf size balls.
  5. Fry them in oil in a pan.
  6. Shake the pan regularly so that the buns are fried on all sides.
  7. Put them carefully into the tomato sauce and let them cook through while cooking the pasta.
  8. Avoid stirring the sauce too much, as the meatballs will break if you mess around too much.

Steps for cooking the pasta

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to package directions.
  2. Be aware that the pasta continues to cook while you pour off the water and mix with the sauce,
    so it must really have bite when you take the pan off the heat.
  3. Pour the sauce and meatballs over the spaghetti in a large dish and eat with lots of freshly grated parmesan.
